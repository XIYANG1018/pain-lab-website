The relationship between oxytocin and empathy for others’ pain: Testing the mediating effect of first-hand pain sensitivity
Chennan Lin, Shiwei Zhuo, Qianqian Zheng, Xiaoyun Li, Weiwei Peng
Physiology & Behavior   ·   01 Oct 2023   ·   doi:10.1016/j.physbeh.2023.114266
The current study explored the relationship between oxytocin, first-hand pain sensitivity, and pain empathy among healthy participants. Oxytocin lessened hyperalgesia to pressure pain due to repeated exposure to painful stimulation, which is indicative of oxytocin-induced attenuation of first-hand pain sensitivity.

Linking interpretation bias to individual differences in pain sensitivity
Zekun Guo, Wenyun Zhang, Xiaohan Lyu, Qian Ye, Weiwei Peng
Current Psychology   ·   22 Oct 2022   ·   doi:10.1007/s12144-022-03793-6
Individual variation in pain sensitivity is related to an interpretation bias toward pain but not to a memory bias. The observed link between pain-sensitivity profiles and interpretation bias in healthy individuals suggests the potential role of interpretation bias in chronic pain development and maintenance. Intervention to reduce interpretation bias toward pain might be helpful in the prevention and treatment of chronic pain.
